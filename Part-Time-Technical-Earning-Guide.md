## Part 4 — Freelancing: step-by-step (Weeks 2–8)

### Step 1 — Pick a niche (narrow wins)

Examples: **API integration**, **Next.js/React fixes**, **AWS deployment**, **Python automation**, **performance tuning**, **code reviews for startups**.  
**Avoid** advertising as “I can do anything”—buyers hire **specific pain relief**.

### Step 2 — Create profiles

1. **[Upwork](https://www.upwork.com/)** — Complete profile, add portfolio links, take relevant skill tests if offered.
2. **[Freelancer.com](https://www.freelancer.com/)** or **[PeoplePerHour](https://www.peopleperhour.com/)** — Optional second channel.
3. **India-focused:** Search for **[Truelancer](https://www.truelancer.com/)**, **[WorknHire](https://www.worknhire.com/)** — volume may be lower; still worth a profile.

### Step 3 — First jobs strategy

1. Apply only to jobs where you can deliver **in under 10–15 hours** at first.
2. **Over-communicate** milestones: “Day 1–2: setup; Day 3–4: feature; Day 5: test & handover.”
3. Ask for a **review** after successful delivery—reviews matter more than rates early on.

### Step 4 — Pricing (rough, India + global clients)

- Early stage: focus on **getting 3–5 completed jobs** with good reviews, not maximum rate.
- Later: move from **hourly** to **fixed milestones** where possible to protect your time.
- For India domestic clients, rates are often lower than US/EU; international clients on Upwork may pay more but expect strong English and async communication.

### Step 5 — Contracts & money

1. Use **platform escrow** when available (e.g., Upwork milestones).
2. For **direct clients:** simple written scope (email or Google Doc signed “Agreed”) + **30–50% upfront** for new clients is common.
3. **Invoicing:** Many use **Razorpay**, **Instamojo**, **PayPal** (for foreign), or bank transfer—choose what matches your client.

### Step 6 — Tax & compliance (high level — verify with a CA)

1. **Freelance income** is taxable in India; keep records of all receipts.
2. If turnover crosses thresholds, **GST** may apply—**consult a chartered accountant**; rules change over time.
3. If you form a **sole proprietorship** or **Pvt Ltd**, get professional advice—don’t guess.

## Part 7 — Daily routine template (4–5 hours)

| Block | Activity |
|--------|----------|
| **0:15** | Check messages from clients/students only; list **3 priorities** for today |
| **2:00–2:30** | Deep work: coding / deliverable |
| **0:15** | Break |

**Rule:** One “admin” day per week (profiles, invoices, learning)—not every day.

---

## Part 8 — Safety (non-negotiable)

1. **Never pay** to get a job (training fees, security deposit, “verification”).
2. **No upfront bank transfers** to “employers”; real clients pay **you** via traceable methods.
3. **NDA / IP:** Read before signing; ensure side work doesn’t steal **employer IP** or **conflict** with your job.
4. **Too-good offers** (very high pay, vague role, Telegram-only hiring) → ignore.

## Part 10 — If you get stuck

| Problem | Action |
|----------|--------|
| No replies on proposals | Narrow niche; improve first paragraph; add video intro (if platform allows) |
| Race to bottom on price | Stop bidding lowest; target **smaller jobs with clear scope** |
| Burnout | Cut to **3 hours/day** until stable; say no to bad clients |
| Client dispute | Use platform mediation; keep **written scope** and **timestamps** |
